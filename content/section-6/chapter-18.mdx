## The Reality Gap

Why does code work in Isaac Sim but fail on a real robot?
1.  **Friction:** Real friction is complex; simulated friction is a simple coefficient.
2.  **Sensor Noise:** Real cameras have motion blur and lighting artifacts.
3.  **Latency:** Real networks have delays; simulation is instantaneous.
4.  **Actuator Dynamics:** Real motors get hot and have backlash.

## Bridging the Gap

### 1. Domain Randomization
Train your AI on 10,000 variations of the world (different lighting, floor friction, object masses). If it works in all of them, it's likely to work in the real world (which is just another variation).

### 2. System Identification
Measure the real robot's properties (mass distribution, motor torque curves) and update the simulation URDF to match exactly.

## Deployment to Jetson

When you are ready to deploy:
1.  **Containerize:** Use Docker to package your ROS 2 workspace.
2.  **Optimize:** Use TensorRT to compile your AI models for the Jetson GPU.
3.  **Safety First:** Always have a physical **Emergency Stop (E-Stop)** button ready.
